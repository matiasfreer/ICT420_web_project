Introduction

My name is Matias, I am 20 years old and I moved to Lexington 6 years ago from my homecountry, Costa Rica.
I am a junior getting a degree in ICT, as well as a minor in Business Analytics.
I chose ICT because of the technology part of it. I have always liked using computers and I always found myself helping family members with them.
I have a very happy life. I like going to the gym and learning new things. I am also a food enthusiast, and my favorite thing to eat are burritos.
My dog's name is Beto(Alberto) and he is an adorable mix between Australian Shepard and a Poodle.

My life in Lexington

After 6 years living in Lexington, I can say I have built a very desirable life here, along with my mom and my dog.
I moved to Lexington when I was 14 years old and in the middle of the pandemic, so my life for the first 2 years was not very happy, I missed my friends and due to the pandemic, I wasn't able to make new ones.
High School was tedious, but I got through it graduating a year earlier, and from that point on, everything went uphill. I was so happy to go into college and make a new life.
Fortunately I made good friends in college and I am very happy with the career that I chose. Now that I am a Junior in college I look back and see how long I've came to be here.
Nowadays, my life is very good, I have a good school-life balance and I follow a routine that I once dreamed of.

My life back in Costa Rica

Back in my homecountry of Costa Rica, there's my family and friends, which I miss everyday. Almost everyone I know is in Costa Rica, and with time I've gotten used to that.
Every once in a while I go back and visit everyone. It always feels like I never left and I have the greatest time with the most important people.
Being in Costa Rica is like living in paradise, the weather is on point, the nature is throbbing everywhere, and people are very kind and humble.
Very often I miss little things like my grandma's cooking, or little treats or cookies I used to eat. But like I said earlier, I try to go once in a while so I get to enjoy everything I sometimes miss.
Although I don't live there anymore, Costa Rica will always be my home, and whenever I decide to go back, it will welcome me with the warmest hug.
